01 · Problem

The multi-model chaos

Today's power users juggle 6 different AI tools. ChatGPT says one thing, Grok says another, DeepSeek something else entirely. No one knows who to trust — and no one has time to find out.

⚡

6 tools, 6 answers

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok, DeepSeek — all say something different. Switching wastes time and focus.

🤷

No way to verify

When models disagree, users have no framework to know whose answer is correct. Gut instinct is not a strategy.

🔁

Manual triangulation

Users copy-paste between tools, re-read six answers, and still can't be confident. They're doing the AI's job.

02 · Solution

How Chorus works

One query. Six frontier models respond. They debate, challenge, and refine each other's answers. Chorus synthesizes one high-confidence response — so you never have to triangulate again.

03 · Product Demo

See Chorus in action

A real example: user asks about the best diet for cognitive performance. All six models deliberate — and Chorus delivers one verified answer.

Chorus · 6-Model Deliberation
✓ Synthesized · 5/6 consensus

User asked: "What is the most evidence-backed diet for long-term cognitive performance and brain health?"

ChatGPT
Mediterranean diet has the strongest RCT evidence base — multiple large trials show reduced cognitive decline over 5+ years.
Claude
MIND diet (Mediterranean + DASH hybrid) shows more targeted brain benefits — emphasis on leafy greens, berries, and omega-3s.
Gemini
Mediterranean is well-supported, but 2024 research also points to time-restricted eating as a powerful cognitive enhancer.
Perplexity
Consensus pick: Mediterranean diet. MIND diet trials show 53% reduction in Alzheimer's risk. Multiple peer-reviewed citations.
Grok
MIND diet aligns with the latest neuroscience literature. Blueberries and walnuts have notably strong individual evidence for cognition.
DeepSeek
Mediterranean and MIND diets both supported. Additionally, ketogenic diet shows short-term cognitive gains — though long-term data is limited.
5/6 agree
ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, and Grok all converge on MIND diet as primary recommendation with Mediterranean as foundation.
DeepSeek challenged
DeepSeek raised ketogenic diet — but majority countered: long-term RCT evidence for keto on cognition remains insufficient vs. MIND.
Grok added
Specific foods within MIND diet (blueberries, walnuts, leafy greens) have independent strong evidence — useful for actionable guidance.
Chorus refined
Keto flagged as "emerging, not yet recommended." Time-restricted eating noted as complementary protocol, not replacement. Strong consensus reached.
Chorus synthesis
The MIND diet (Mediterranean-DASH Intervention for Neurodegenerative Delay) has the strongest combined evidence for long-term cognitive health. Prioritize leafy greens, blueberries, walnuts, fish, and olive oil. Time-restricted eating (16:8) may complement as a secondary protocol. Keto shows short-term promise but lacks long-term RCT support — not yet recommended as primary strategy.
✓ Accuracy: High
5/6 models in consensus
Sources: PREDIMED, MIND Trial, Lancet 2024
